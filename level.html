How Your Level Works

Your Rally level is a number between 1.0 and 7.0. It's assigned after a short skill assessment when you join, and it gets more accurate every time you play a match. The better we know your game, the better Rally works for you.

Padel Skill Levels

Tier Range What it means
🟤 D2 1.0 – 1.9 Just picking up a racket — first steps on court
🟤 D1 2.0 – 2.4 Learning the basics, still building fundamentals
🟤 C2 2.5 – 2.9 Getting comfortable — consistent rallies, starting to feel the game
⚪ C1 3.0 – 3.4 Solid recreational player — knows the positions, reads the game
⚪ B2 3.5 – 3.9 Competitive club player — tactical awareness, reliable under pressure
🟡 B1 4.0 – 4.9 High club / low competitive — strong all-round game, tournament experience
🟡 A2 5.0 – 5.9 Competitive tournament player — technical and tactical
🟡 A1 6.0 – 7.0 Elite level — national or professional circuit

Levels are grouped into three tiers: Bronze (1.0–2.9), Silver (3.0–3.9), and Gold (4.0+). Most recreational club players fall in the Silver range.

How Your Initial Level Is Assigned

When you first join Rally, you complete a short skill assessment — 13 questions that take about 2 minutes. There are no right or wrong answers; the more honestly you respond, the more accurately your level reflects your actual game.

The assessment covers three areas:

🏋️ Athletic Foundation

Your general sports background — how long you've played racket sports, how active you currently are, your physical fitness, and your natural sporting aptitude. This area carries more weight for players who are new to padel, since your athletic base gives the clearest signal.

🎾 Padel Experience

How much padel you've actually played — your frequency, total volume, comfort on the glass, and your own honest assessment of your level. Players who've been playing for years will score differently here than those just starting out.

🏆 Tactical & Competitive

Whether you understand court positioning, how you'd handle key game situations, and your tournament history — how many competitions you've entered and how far you've gone. This pillar matters most for experienced players.

Smart weighting

The algorithm adapts to your profile. If you've never played padel before, your athletic background carries more weight — because there's simply more signal there. If you've never entered a tournament, that weight is redistributed across the other areas. The system always works with what it knows.

Rally also runs consistency checks across your answers. If you rate yourself highly but show limited tactical knowledge, the self-assessment is moderated automatically. This makes the initial level more reliable for everyone.

How Your Level Evolves with Matches

Your questionnaire level is your starting point — but it's not fixed. Every completed match in Rally feeds into your match rating, which updates after every game.

After each match, the system compares the result against what was statistically expected, based on both teams' ratings. Beating a stronger team moves your level up more than beating a weaker one. Losing to a stronger team barely affects you — that outcome was expected.

In doubles, the system accounts for your partner. If you're paired with a significantly stronger player, your individual contribution is weighted accordingly — so your rating reflects your own performance, not just whether your team won.

As you play more matches, your match history gradually takes over from your initial assessment:

Matches played Influence of match results
0 0% — pure questionnaire
5 ~71%
10 ~92%
20+ ~99%

After around 10–15 matches, your level is almost entirely driven by your actual results. The questionnaire gets you started — your game keeps you honest.

Why Accuracy Matters

Your level is the primary signal Rally uses to recommend suitable partners and opponents.

When your level is accurate, Rally can surface players who are genuinely well-matched with you — close enough that games are competitive, far enough that you're always learning. For club owners, accurate levels mean better-organized open matches, fairer draws, and players who come back because they had a great game on court.

The more you play, the better your level reflects your real game — and the better Rally gets at connecting you with the right people.
